# The Autonomous Growth System | Framework Overview
## Competing in the Post-Marketing Era
**By Malin Gaertig** | April ‘26

### Introduction: The End of the Attention Economy
Digital growth has been driven by human attention for three decades. Today, autonomous agents are becoming the new decision-makers. In this landscape, decisions are determined by structured data and operational performance rather than persuasion or storytelling.

### The Three Laws of Autonomous Growth
1. **Law 1: Visibility Is Determined by Machine Readability.** If machines cannot interpret your data, you do not exist.
2. **Law 2: Preference Is Determined by Verifiable Performance.** Measurable outcomes replace perceived value.
3. **Law 3: Growth Emerges From System Integration.** Growth is an emergent property of integrated organizational systems.

### The Four Pillars of AGS
1. **Agentic Commerce:** The interface for machine discovery and transaction.
2. **Operational Alpha:** Measurable capabilities (fulfillment, pricing) that shape machine preference.
3. **Personalised AI Agents:** Internal systems for dynamic response and external distribution.
4. **Intelligent Governance:** Policies and data standards ensuring programmable trust.

### Measuring Agentic Readiness (ARS)
Agentic readiness is evaluated across five domains:
1. Machine-readable discovery.
2. Autonomous decision capability.
3. Transactional execution.
4. Continuous learning.
5. Governance and trust infrastructure.

### The Maturity Model
- **Phase 1: Attention Economy**: Human-mediated markets where success is constrained by cognitive bandwidth. Primary KPI: Conversion Rate (CR%).
- **Phase 2: Machine-Mediated Interfaces**: AI-assisted decision layers where brands compete for machine visibility through signals. Primary KPI: Agent Visibility.
- **Phase 3: Autonomous Economy**: System-to-system growth where revenue is decoupled from media spend and headcount. Primary KPI: Machine Preference Index (MPI).
