# AGS System Architecture (domain.md)

## Core Philosophy: The Post-Marketing Era
Growth has shifted from **Attention Capture** (persuasion-led) to **Decision Qualification** (logic-led). The primary customer is no longer a human browsing a screen, but an autonomous agent performing high-speed evaluations.

## The Three Laws of Autonomous Growth
1. **Visibility is determined by machine readability**: If machines cannot interpret your data, you do not exist.
2. **Preference is determined by verifiable performance**: Measurable reliability replaces perceived value.
3. **Growth emerges from total system integration**: Silos are the enemy; growth is an architectural capability.

## The Four Pillars of AGS
1. **Agentic Commerce (The Transaction Engine)**: Machine-readable interfaces (WebMCP/UCP) for autonomous discovery and execution.
2. **Operational Alpha (The Source of Advantage)**: Real-time, verifiable performance signals (pricing, fulfilment) that drive machine selection.
3. **Personalised AI Agents (The Distribution Layer)**: Internal and external agents managing intent and operational response.
4. **Intelligent Governance (The Trust Layer)**: Programmable trust, data integrity, and identity verification.

## Key Metrics & Success Indicators
- **MPI (Machine Preference Index)**: Objective measure of selection probability within a model.
- **SoM (Share of Model)**: Percentage of recommendations within an AI model's decision weights.
- **CPAA (Cost Per Agent Acquisition)**: Total investment in data infrastructure and signal integrity required to become "selectable."
- **ARS (Agentic Readiness Score)**: Benchmark of an organisation's maturity across discovery, decisioning, and execution.
